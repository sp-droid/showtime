Tiramisù

/'Pick me up'/ The tiramisù is an Italian dessert that combines dry biscuits soaked in coffee with a mascarpone cheese and egg cream, and a note of bitter cocoa on top. It's one of the world's most famous desserts, and with reason; it's both extremely simple to make and delicious.


Origin

1960-70s Treviso, Italy.


Ingredients for 8 servings


Cream

  • 75g white sugar
  • 3 egg yolks
  • 250g mascarpone (>40% fat. Mila is a good brand, sweet, full of flavor and quite yellow)

Layering

  • Coffee
  • Bitter cocoa powder
  • 12 Savoiardi (ladyfingers. Basically 4 or 5 per layer up to 3 layers, depending on the size of the container)


Utensils


<
>
Hand mixer
Airtight container
Fridge


Steps


Brew coffee and set aside

Making the cream

  1. Whip the yolks (whites are excess) with the sugar until it develops soft peaks. Mix in 1 tsp of the brewed coffee.
  2. Incorporate the mascarpone, whip slowly then faster for 3 minutes. Cream shouldn't be pourable, but consistent.

Layering

  1. Spread thin layer of cream on the bottom to avoid coffee leaks.
  2. Dip the ladyfingers once on the coffee, quickly, drain them vertically and place them.
  3. Add cream until the Savoiardi are hidden, repeat for 3 layers of ladyfingers. With each layer, turn them 90º.
  4. Finally, add cacao powder on top.

8h rest in the fridge



Variations


  • Substitute the Savoiardi for pan di Spagna (bizcocho, Spanish sponge cake)

CULINARY TIP

Using these quantities, I've tried 4 layers of 4 Savoiardi each, but in my opinion it's better to stay at 3: the cream layers become thicker, and the Tiramisù stays perfectly balanced.

CULINARY TIP

Coffee from an Espresso machine, and even from a well brewed Moka, is too strong. Dilute respectively with x3 and x1 as much water as there is coffee.

CULINARY TIP

Be sure to learn how to brew coffee in a Moka without bitterness for this recipe. It makes a world of difference.

CULINARY TIP

It's a sin to throw away the whites! Conserve them in the fridge. They are high in protein and can be added to scrambled eggs, used for Amaretti biscuits, etc.

SERVING TIP

Tiramisù is best served as squares or rectangles.
