Tartaletas dulces

/'Sweet tartlets'/ Bite-sized pastries, filled with a variety of ingredients and open on top. Though first seen in older times, sweet tartlets came to popularity with the spread of sugar in medieval Europe


Origin

Medieval western Europe, France-England


Ingredients for 20 servings


Base

  • 200g flour
  • 60g sugar
  • 100g butter (salted may be ok, though I would go with unsalted and adding a pinch of salt instead)
  • 1 egg
  • 0.5 tsp baking powder
  • Moulds (paper or metal)
  • Vanilla (optional)

Filling

  • Depends, check below


Utensils


<
>
Oven
Rolling pin
Piping bag


Steps


Base

  1. Mix flour, sugar and cold butter until well combined. Now add egg, vanilla and knead until well mixed. Put it in the fridge 1h.
  2. Using a rolling pin and sprinkling some flour if needed, extend the dough well, aiming for a 0.5-1mm thickness
  3. Preheat oven to 180ºC
  4. Meanwhile cut the dough sheet in circular portions and press them inside each mould. You can use the edge of a glass to press and cut evenly sized portions. Poke the bottoms with a fork.
  5. Bake for 20-25'. Take out and add filling

Filling ideas

Dulce de leche: Boil under water a can of condensed milk, for 3h to get caramel-like color.

Caramel au Beurre Salé, nutella...

Jams, jellies

Fresh fruits

Special creams and custards: lemon curd, creme patisserie, crema diplomatica, stabilized whipped cream...

Shavings or dusting on top, from i.e. chocolate, cacao, cocoa, cinnamon

or a combination of any of the above.


Lattice crust top

If you plan on filling with jams, a good option is to make thin dough stripes to form a lattice pattern on top

CULINARY TIP

When piping the filling, you can decorate as much as you want, but beware if it's not thick enough it will slowly lose shape.

CULINARY TIP

Very humid fillings like cream may make the tartlet soggy on the bottom. Glazing it with jam first helps it remain dry.
