Albóndigas en salsa

/'Meatballs in sauce'/


Origin

300s Rome, Ancient Rome. (De recoquinaria, Apicius)


Ingredients for 3 servings


Meatballs

  • 250 g minced beef
  • 250 g minced pork
  • 1 egg
  • 2 slices of sandwich bread (without the crust)
  • Milk
  • 1 garlic clove (minced)
  • Salt
  • Pepper
  • Flour

The rest

  • 2 garlic cloves (cut)
  • 2 onions
  • 2 carrots
  • 3 ripe tomatoes (or equivalent)
  • Quarter glass of white wine
  • Salt
  • Sweet paprika
  • Bay leaf
  • Thyme
  • 2 tbsp olive oil


Utensils


<
>
Kitchen pot
Pan


Steps


The first two steps can and should be prepared at the same time.


Tomatoes

  1. On a pot, put a tiny bit of oil with the tomatoes cut in half, cover and place on a low-medium heat.
  2. After some minutes the tomatoes are soft and they are to be mashed with a fork, and left cooking on low.

Meatballs

  1. To a beaten egg add pepper, (quite a bit of) salt, a minced clove of garlic, mix well and add the meat. Shred the bread, slightly soak in milk and mix in with the rest.
  2. Add the 2 tbsp of oil on a pan and start heating on medium.
  3. Using the measure of your choice (i.e. a spoon) make evenly sized meatballs, cover in flour (shake the extra flour) and brown them on at least 2 sides.
  4. When they are done, add them to the tomato sauce, which by now can be turned off. Also remove any chunks left in the oil.

Fritura

  1. Deglaze the pan with a very tiny splash of the wine.
  2. Add the finely chopped onions, brown them. Sweetness is often an issue in this recipe and depends on the tomatoes, onion browning and if you decide to correct with sugar.
  3. Add salt, finely chopped garlic, sliced carrots and cook on medium heat for at least 10-12'.
  4. Pour in the wine, wait until it evaporates completely and turn the heat off. Now move everything to the pot.

Finishing

  1. Pour water until the meatballs are almost submerged, add a tsp of paprika, the bay leaf, a dusting of thyme and leave for 45'-1h on low-medium heat, half-covered.
  2. Correct for salt mid way, and check there is enough sauce or else add water.


Variations


  • I read once about an Italian grandma who added 1 or 2 seedless raisins to each meatball. I simply add them to the mix after soaking them in water for a few minutes.
  • To improve thickness and texture, adding about 4 cashews to the tomato sauce helps. Remove them after a while, blend them very finely with a bit of water and pour it back in.
