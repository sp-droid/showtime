Fabada asturiana

/'Asturian beans'/


Origin

1500-1600 Asturias, Spain


Ingredients for 2 servings


  • 80 g pancetta (1 tocino)
  • 80 g morcilla asturiana (1 small)
  • 80 g chorizo asturiano (1 small)
  • 250 g dry beans (fabas)
  • 12 g olive oil (1 tbsp)
  • 0.5 onion (or 1 small)
  • 1 clove of garlic (1 small)
  • Sweet paprika (1 tsp)
  • A few strands of saffron
  • Salt


Utensils


<
>
Kitchen pot


Steps


  1. Cover the beans and saffron in water the night before. They absorb a lot of water, do make sure they have extra.
  2. The day after, put the peeled onion, clove of garlic and beans covered with water (use the one from the night before). Bring to a boil.
  3. Now we add a splash of cold water to stop the boiling, and boil again. we do this twice more. It's called 'scaring' the beans.
  4. Add everything else except the salt and cook on low-medium heat for 2-3 hours. After this time correct for salt.


Variations


  • The fully traditional recipe is the compango (tocino, morcilla, chorizo), water, salt and beans. But it varies from home to home, and as the dish travelled it gained a few additions. In my opinion they don't detract from the original flavour, as long as we add them in little quantities.
