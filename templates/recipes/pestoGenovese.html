Pesto Genovese

/'Genoese grounded paste'/ Although the first written recipe dates to 1852, very similar dishes can be found dating back to Roman times, but closer enactments were more prevalent in the Liguria region.


Origin

1852 Genoa, Italy. (Emanuele Rossi)


Ingredients for 4 servings


Pesto

  • 30 g fresh basil leaves
  • 12.9 g grated parmigiano reggiano / cured cheese (cow)
  • 4.3 g grated pecorino / cured cheese (sheep)
  • 1 clove of garlic
  • 5 g pine nuts
  • 48 g olive oil (4 tbsp)
  • Pinch of salt

Pasta

  • 440 g pasta
  • Salt


Utensils


<
>
Hand blender
Airtight container
Fridge


Steps


Blend ingredients together until it forms a green paste and save in an airtight container


Boil the pasta, dry out and mix with the pesto.



Variations


  • Substitute the pine nuts for pistachios or walnuts.

CULINARY TIP

The more slender of a container, the better, as it reduces the area of pesto in contact with the air.

CULINARY TIP

I find that pesto improves on the day after. After that it's worse however: it slowly oxidizes and loses color.
