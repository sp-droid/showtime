Tiramis첫

The tiramis첫 is an Italian dessert that combines dry biscuits soaked in coffee with a mascarpone cheese and egg cream, and a note of bitter cocoa on top. It's one of the world's most famous desserts, and with reason; it's both extremely simple to make and delicious.


Origin

1960-70s Treviso, Italy.


Ingredients for 8 servings


Cream

  • 75g white sugar
  • 3 egg yolks
  • 250g mascarpone (>40% fat)

Layering

  • Coffee
  • Bitter cocoa powder
  • 15 Savoiardi


Utensils


Hand mixer
Airtight container
Fridge


Steps


Brew coffee and set aside

Making the cream

  1. Whip the yolks (whites are excess) with the sugar until it develops soft peaks.
  2. Incorporate the mascarpone, whip slowly then faster for 3 minutes. Cream shouldn't be pourable, but consistent.

Layering

  1. Spread thin layer of cream on the bottom to avoid coffee leaks.
  2. Dip the ladyfingers once on the coffee, quickly, drain them vertically and place them.
  3. Add cream until the ladyfingers are hidden, repeat.
  4. Finally, add cacao powder on top.

8h rest in the fridge



Variants

['Substitute the Savoiardi for pan di Spagna (bizcocho, Spanish sponge cake)']

CULINARY TIP

It's a sin to throw away the whites! Conserve them in the fridge. They are high in protein and can be added to scrambled eggs, used for Amaretti biscuits, etc.

SERVING TIP

Tiramis첫 is best served as squares or rectangles.
