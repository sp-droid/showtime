Arroz con leche

/'Rice with milk'/


Origin

3000-250 BC, Asia.


Ingredients for 4 servings


  • 700 ml whole milk
  • 50 g white rice (no basmati, no flavoured rice of any kind, not the long kind)
  • 40 g sugar
  • Tsp of butter
  • Tsp or a stick of cinnamon
  • A couple of orange peels


Utensils


<
>
Kitchen pot


Steps


If you don't have dry fruit skin, you might want to do it at least a day before so it dries and it becomes less bitter. Take extreme care of not getting absolutely any pith (the white stuff) so the peel should be very thin. Otherwise the pudding will contract a bitter taste. They can be left to eat or removed after boiling the milk.

  1. With the help of a glass (using a strainer wastes water) wash the rice until the water comes off more or less clear.
  2. In a pot pour the milk along with the fruit peel and sugar on medium to high heat. When it starts to boil add the rice and lower the heat. It will bubble, sometimes a lot. Stirring with a fork helps, but don't be afraid to take the pot out of the stove if it's about to overflow. It will subside eventually.
  3. The goal now is to cook on extremely low heat, to a slight boil, from 1.5 to 2 h.
  4. In a plate mix using a fork the butter and cinnamon to form a paste, then add it to the rice.

Unfortunately, cooking this dish requires frequent attention. Every 15-20 minutes, break the top layer, check the bottom isn't sticking but don't stir. Once it's done the milk will have changed color, it will be thicker in consistency, but still have liquid. Turn off heat and cover.



Variations


  • Use peels from different fruits like apples.
  • Explore how they make rice pudding in other countries.

CULINARY TIP

I meant it when I wrote 'extremely low'. After a while it becomes so dense and good at storing heat that it easily burns. In my case when I do it over gas I have to put the pot inside a larger empty one.

SERVING TIP

Serve in small ceramic glasses, with a dash of cinnamon on top to decorate.
